MSS Calculator is a dashboard program to visualize the status of various metrics and calculate the minimum sample size required to perform an A/B Test.

It takes the Minimum Detectable Effect, the Base Conversion Rate (Lift) and Ramp Date to calculate Minimum Sample Size, Number of Weeks required for the experiment and the Number of Activations per day/per year/to the end of the Year for each individual Metric.

The Different Metrics are:

  • RP - Raw Prospectors:
    Visitors who haven't spent much time on the website.

  • QP - Qualified Pospectors:
    Visitors who have spent some time looking around the website.

  • SS - Signup Starts:
    Visitors who have started the Signup process.

  • SC - Signup Complete:
    Visitors who have completed the Signup process.

  • Activations:
    Visitors who have Activated their Subscription.

  • 7d - Visitors who have been to the website over a period of 7 days.

    The Minimum Sample Size is calculated by the following formula

Sample Size = (Zα/2 ×√(2p1×(1−p1))+√(Zβ×p1×(1−p1)+p2×(1−p2)))2 / (|p2−p1|)2

Where:

  • p1 is the "Baseline conversion rate".

  • p2 is the conversion rate lifted by "Minimum Detectable Effect", which means p1 + Minimum Detectable Effect.

  • α is the "Significance level α".

  • β is the β in "Statistical power 1−β".

  • Zα/2 means Z Score from the z table that corresponds to α/2.

  • Zβ means Z Score from the z table that corresponds to β

The values chosen are:

    α=5%

    β=20%

The weeks required are calculated using the average conversion rates for different Metrics in 2 weeks. Similarly the activations is also calculated using the average activations over a period of 2 weeks.
